Biography

Rafsun Hossnain is the founder and CEO of Troyva, where he identified the campus fragmentation problem firsthand as a student navigating dozens of unverified group chats across Dhaka's university ecosystem. He built Troyva's complete product vision, brand system, investor site, app prototype, and pitch materials before recruiting a co-founder. His approach combines analytical rigor from a pure science background (Cambridge A-Levels in Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry with a perfect 4.0 GPA) with direct campus network access as president and coordinator of multiple student organizations. He has led teams at the international level as Engineering & Finance Team Lead for NASA's Human Exploration Rover Challenge 2025, where his team placed 9th globally, and has won recognition at the International Greenwich Olympiad (Silver Medal) for primary research on child marriage in Bangladesh — findings that were shared with UNICEF and Plan International.

Rafsun brings a rare combination of research discipline, business acumen, and ground-level understanding of the South Asian student market. His professional experience spans research (Child Health Research Foundation) and business operations (AVA Enterprise Ltd.). Beyond his professional pursuits, Rafsun founded the Sarbosheba Foundation, a village-level community service organisation, and built a free village library with personal savings for underserved readers in rural Bangladesh.

Biography

Abrar Mahmud Hasan is the co-founder and CTO of Troyva, bringing production-grade engineering experience and a deep conviction that AI should be a core component of observable, fault-tolerant infrastructure. As a CS student at Islamic University of Technology (CGPA: 3.80/4.00), he has already built and shipped real-world software at scale across multiple industries. Before joining Troyva, Abrar served as a Software Engineering Intern at Intelsense AI, designing dashboards and agentic interfaces translating complex data into actionable business intelligence. Prior to that, he worked as a Junior Software Engineer at Shahrish Engineering & Construction Limited, where he developed internal supply-chain modules that reduced manual data handling by 90%.

His personal project Valerix — a Kubernetes-orchestrated microservices platform demonstrating service resilience, controlled failures, and production-style cloud deployment on AWS EKS — reflects the exact infrastructure philosophy Troyva needs as it scales globally. He is Champion of the Microservice & DevOps Hackathon at BUET CSE Fest 2026.
